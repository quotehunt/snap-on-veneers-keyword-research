# Methodology

This document explains how the VeneerVibe snap-on veneers keyword research dataset is structured, grouped, and interpreted.

The purpose of this repository is to help understand how U.S. consumers search for snap-on veneers, pop-on veneers, clip-on veneers, and removable veneers before making a purchase decision.

This is not a clinical study, dental recommendation, or medical dataset. It is a search-intent and content-research resource.

## Research Purpose

The main goal is to identify the practical questions people ask before buying removable veneers.

These questions usually fall into areas such as:

- Cost
- Reviews
- Brand comparisons
- Missing teeth suitability
- Eating and drinking
- Safety
- Gum health
- Appearance and realism
- Refund policies
- Warranty coverage
- Impression kits
- Fit issues
- Discount codes
- Payment plans

By grouping these searches, VeneerVibe can create clearer buyer education around removable veneers.

## Core Search Categories

The research focuses on several overlapping terms used by consumers.

These include:

- Snap-on veneers
- Pop-on veneers
- Clip-on veneers
- Removable veneers
- Snap-on teeth veneers
- Clip-on teeth veneers
- Press-on veneers
- Temporary veneers
- Custom removable veneers
- Snap-on smile alternatives

Many consumers use these terms interchangeably, even though products, brands, materials, fit, warranty terms, and intended use can vary significantly.

## Data Source Notes

The keyword files in this repository are based on exported keyword research data around removable veneer searches in the United States.

The data may include:

- Keyword phrase
- Country
- Difficulty estimate
- Search volume estimate where available
- Related keyword variations

Keyword research tools provide estimates, not exact search numbers. Search volume, competition, and difficulty can change over time.

Factors that may affect search demand include:

- Social media trends
- Paid advertising
- Brand campaigns
- Seasonal events
- Consumer reviews
- News coverage
- Search engine updates
- Product pricing changes
- Discount campaigns

## Keyword Grouping Method

Keywords are grouped by search intent rather than exact wording.

For example, these queries belong to the same cost-intent group:

- snap on veneers cost
- snap on veneers price
- how much are snap on veneers
- how much do snap on veneers cost
- affordable snap on veneers

These queries belong to the same eating and daily-use group:

- can you eat with pop on veneers
- eating with snap on veneers
- can you chew with pop on veneers
- can you drink coffee with snap on veneers

These queries belong to the missing teeth group:

- snap on veneers for missing teeth
- pop on veneers with missing teeth
- best snap on veneers for missing teeth
- can pop on veneers replace missing teeth

This method helps reveal what stage of the buying journey the searcher may be in.

## Funnel Interpretation

Searches can be broadly grouped into three funnel stages.

### Top of Funnel

These searchers are learning about the category.

Examples:

- what are snap on veneers
- fake teeth explained
- removable veneers
- fix missing teeth without implants

These users usually need simple education, definitions, pros and cons, and safety guidance.

### Middle of Funnel

These searchers are comparing options and checking suitability.

Examples:

- snap on veneers for missing teeth
- are snap on veneers safe
- can you eat with pop on veneers
- do snap on veneers look fake

These users usually need practical buying guidance, comparison tables, warnings, and expectation-setting.

### Bottom of Funnel

These searchers are close to purchase.

Examples:

- best snap on veneers
- pop on veneers reviews
- shiny smile vs trusmile
- pop on veneers discount code
- snap on veneers refund policy

These users usually need brand comparisons, review analysis, warranty details, pricing, and clear next steps.

## Editorial Use

VeneerVibe uses this research to create consumer-focused content that answers real buyer questions.

Priority is given to content that helps users understand:

- What removable veneers can and cannot do
- Which buyers may be suitable
- Who should avoid them
- How much they cost
- Whether they look natural
- Whether they can be used with missing teeth
- What to expect from impression kits
- What happens if they do not fit
- Which brands offer better value, warranty, or support

The goal is to improve clarity, not to push every visitor toward buying.

## Important Limitations

This dataset has several limitations.

### Search Volume Is Estimated

Keyword search volume is not exact. It should be used directionally.

### Brand Policies Change

Pricing, warranties, discounts, remake policies, refund terms, and product materials can change over time.

### Search Intent Can Overlap

A query such as "pop on veneers reviews" may indicate research, skepticism, purchase intent, or complaint research.

### Search Data Is Not Medical Evidence

Search behavior shows what consumers ask. It does not prove product safety, clinical effectiveness, or suitability.

### Some Terms Are Brand Names

Terms such as "Pop On Veneers" may refer to a specific brand, while many consumers also use similar wording generically. Content should be careful not to confuse brand names with general product categories.

## Consumer Safety Principle

VeneerVibe treats removable veneers as cosmetic appliances, not replacements for professional dental care.

Anyone with the following should speak with a licensed dental professional before ordering removable veneers:

- Loose teeth
- Bleeding gums
- Gum disease
- Tooth pain
- Untreated decay
- Active infection
- Severe bite issues
- Jaw pain or TMJ symptoms
- Multiple missing teeth
- Very few remaining natural teeth
- A history of grinding or clenching

## Recommended Update Frequency

This research should be reviewed periodically because removable veneer search demand can change quickly.

Recommended update schedule:

- Review keyword data every 3 to 6 months
- Review brand pricing every 1 to 3 months
- Review warranty and refund policies every 1 to 3 months
- Review buyer-intent clusters after major Google updates
- Review emerging social media trends monthly
